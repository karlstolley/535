Instructional Design

A graduate course in designing learning experiences for adults. Taught by Karl Stolley, Fall Semester 2012 at Illinois Institute of Technology.

Course Calendar

Week One August 21, 2012

  • Agenda

    1. Welcome & introductions
    2. Log into Campfire (you should have received an invite in your @hawk.iit.edu email; get in the habit of logging into Campfire first thing, every night of class)
    3. Course policies and instructional approach
    4. Sign up for a GitHub account (note: you may use an alias for this class; you’ll do many things with this account, including editing the course wiki)
    5. Setting up course tech (Git, GitHub keys, oh-my-zsh)
    6. Introduce The Project & assign subject areas
    7. Learning experience: Twitter Bootstrap

  • Assigned

    1. Read John Dewey’s Experience and Education (PDF)
    2. Read Mastering the Instructional Design Process (MIDP), Preface to the Fourth Edition & Chapters 1-3
    3. Read Getting Real (GR; from Chapters 1 & 4):
      • What Is Getting Real?
      • What’s the big idea?
      • Ignore Details Early On
      • It’s a Problem When It’s a Problem
      • Make Opinionated Software

        Note: Throughout Getting Real, when you see the word software or web application, mentally replace it with “project.”

    4. Research your subject area for The Project; begin assembling a list of other instructional resources that you find useful to work through and post them to the wiki
